About Me

Hi! My name is Bill Shauck. I have a degree in Computer Science from Yale University. After graduation I worked 8-9 years in the Bay Area for Adobe, Xerox, and a startup spun out from the Xerox Palo Alto Research Center dedicated to making Smalltalk commercially viable. I spent an additional 8+ years providing training and consulting at major firms like Texas Instruments, FedEx, Chase, Lockheed, and Sprint. I've demonstrated wide areas of expertise, from creating ROM BIOS routines; writing Document Assembly functions for a commercial word processor; co-developing a C-based virtual machine (or VM) that outperformed a hand-crafted assembly language VM on the same platforms; object-oriented analysis, design, and implementation for several frameworks and applications; analysis of telecom networks and management systems outlining multiple possible architectural approaches for the then impending US West merger with Qwest; UI work for Lockheed's Performance Management System (revolutionizing project management for Government contracts worth over $10 million); and pioneering the Service Assurance prototype for Sprint's Integrated On-demand Network. I learned additional techniques for problem-solving and client communication during a healthcare field interlude. I chose App Academy as an intensive, immersive half-year bootcamp to demonstrate my continued ability to learn current languages, frameworks, and development approaches in the Web full-stack arena.
